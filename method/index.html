The Method

A step-by-step account of how we measure elemental abundances — from raw HARPS spectra to calibrated [X/H] ratios with formal Type A and Type B uncertainties.

○ Full methodology doc in preparation — RYA-74

Six-step pipeline overview

The Codex uses a reproducible, open-source Python pipeline built around ATLAS9/Castelli-Kurucz model atmospheres and HARPS high-resolution spectroscopy. The methodology PDF will document every decision with citation-level precision.

  • 01
    Spectrum acquisition & normalization
    HARPS archive retrieval, continuum normalization using iterative sigma-clipping spline fit, S/N assessment per order.
  • 02
    Stellar parameters
    Teff from excitation equilibrium, log g from ionization equilibrium (Fe I/II), microturbulence from EW vs. abundance trend. Iterated to convergence.
  • 03
    Line list loading
    VALD3 + NIST master CSV, filtered by quality grade (A+/A preferred). Blend-flagged lines excluded from abundance determination.
  • 04
    Equivalent width measurement
    Gaussian fitting to absorption profiles. Profiles with χ²/ν > 2 flagged. EW range 20–200 mÅ retained for curve-of-growth analysis.
  • 05
    Abundance derivation
    MOOG-style curve of growth analysis. NLTE corrections applied for Fe, O, Na, Mg following Amarsi et al. (2020). Solar reference: Asplund et al. (2021).
  • 06
    Uncertainty budget & ratios
    Type A: line-to-line scatter (σ/√N). Type B: stellar parameter sensitivity (ΔTeff=50K, Δlog g=0.1, Δξ=0.1 km/s). C/O, Mg/Si, and alpha/Fe ratios with propagated uncertainties.
