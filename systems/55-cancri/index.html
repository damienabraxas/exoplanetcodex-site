About this system

Why 55 Cancri A?

55 Cancri A is the most scientifically interesting nearby exoplanet host star available. Its innermost planet, Janssen (55 Cnc e), was observed by JWST in 2024 and showed a possible CO₂-rich atmosphere — the first direct atmospheric detection of a rocky super-Earth. What that atmosphere is made of depends partly on what the star itself is made of.

The C/O ratio of 55 Cancri A remains contested in the literature. Some teams find it elevated above solar (suggesting carbon-rich interior models for the planets). We intend to resolve this with HARPS spectra at R~115,000 and a formal Type A + Type B uncertainty budget.

Full abundance table will publish here when the analysis completes. All raw data and pipeline code are open on GitHub.
